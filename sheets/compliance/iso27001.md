# ISO 27001 (Information Security Management System)

The international standard for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an Information Security Management System (ISMS), using the Plan-Do-Check-Act cycle, with the 2022 revision restructuring Annex A from 114 controls in 14 domains to 93 controls in 4 themes.

## ISMS Fundamentals
### Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) Cycle
```
PLAN — Establish the ISMS
  - Define scope and boundaries
  - Establish information security policy
  - Conduct risk assessment
  - Develop risk treatment plan
  - Select controls (Annex A)
  - Prepare Statement of Applicability (SoA)
  - Obtain management approval

DO — Implement and operate the ISMS
  - Implement risk treatment plan
  - Deploy selected controls
  - Define metrics and measurements
  - Implement training and awareness
  - Manage operations and resources
  - Implement incident detection and response

CHECK — Monitor and review the ISMS
  - Execute monitoring procedures
  - Conduct internal audits (Clause 9.2)
  - Perform management review (Clause 9.3)
  - Record results and actions
  - Measure control effectiveness
  - Review risk assessment at planned intervals

ACT — Maintain and improve the ISMS
  - Implement corrective actions
  - Address nonconformities
  - Communicate improvements
  - Apply lessons learned
  - Continual improvement (Clause 10.2)
```

## Risk Assessment Methodology
### Process per Clause 6.1.2
```bash
# Risk assessment process
cat <<'EOF' > risk_assessment.yaml
step_1_identify_assets:
  - information_assets: "Data, databases, documentation"
  - software_assets: "Applications, system software, tools"
  - physical_assets: "Hardware, facilities, equipment"
  - services: "Computing, communications, utilities"
  - people: "Staff, contractors, third parties"
  - intangibles: "Reputation, brand, intellectual property"

step_2_identify_threats:
  natural: "Flood, earthquake, fire, lightning"
  deliberate: "Hacking, theft, sabotage, espionage"
  accidental: "Human error, equipment failure, software bugs"
  environmental: "Power failure, water damage, contamination"

step_3_identify_vulnerabilities:
  technical: "Unpatched software, weak authentication"
  organizational: "Lack of policies, poor training"
  physical: "Inadequate access control, poor location"
  personnel: "Insufficient screening, lack of awareness"

step_4_assess_likelihood:
  scale: [1_very_low, 2_low, 3_medium, 4_high, 5_very_high]

step_5_assess_impact:
  dimensions: [confidentiality, integrity, availability]
  scale: [1_negligible, 2_minor, 3_moderate, 4_major, 5_severe]

step_6_calculate_risk:
  formula: "Risk = Likelihood x Impact"
  risk_matrix_5x5:
    acceptable: "1-6"
    tolerable: "7-12"
    unacceptable: "13-25"

step_7_risk_treatment:
  options:
    - modify: "Apply controls to reduce risk"
    - accept: "Accept risk within appetite (management approval)"
    - avoid: "Cease the activity causing risk"
    - share: "Transfer risk (insurance, outsourcing)"
EOF
```

## Statement of Applicability (SoA)
### Structure and Purpose
```
The SoA is a mandatory document (Clause 6.1.3 d)):

For each of the 93 Annex A controls, document:
  1. Whether the control is applicable (yes/no)
  2. Justification for inclusion or exclusion
  3. Implementation status (implemented, partial, planned)
  4. Control description / reference to procedure
  5. Risk treatment plan reference

The SoA serves as:
  - Bridge between risk assessment and control implementation
  - Central reference for auditors
  - Evidence of due diligence in control selection
  - Scope definition for certification

Common pitfall: Excluding controls without justification.
Even controls that seem irrelevant must have a documented
reason for exclusion (e.g., "No removable media used — policy
prohibits, verified by technical controls").
```

## Certification Process
### Stage 1 and Stage 2 Audits
```bash
cat <<'EOF'
Pre-Assessment (Optional):
  - Gap analysis against ISO 27001 requirements
  - Identifies major nonconformities before formal audit
  - Not part of the certification — informal review
  - Typically 1-3 months before Stage 1

Stage 1 Audit (Documentation Review):
  Duration: 1-3 days (depending on scope)
  Focus:
    - ISMS scope and boundaries
    - Information security policy
    - Risk assessment and treatment plan
    - Statement of Applicability
    - Internal audit results
    - Management review records
    - Mandatory documented procedures
  Output:
    - Stage 1 report
    - Findings and observations
    - Confirmation of readiness for Stage 2
  Minimum gap: 1-3 months between Stage 1 and Stage 2

Stage 2 Audit (Implementation Audit):
  Duration: 5-15 days (based on scope, size, complexity)
  Focus:
    - Control implementation effectiveness
    - Evidence of PDCA operation
    - Staff interviews and awareness verification
    - Process observation
    - Record review (incidents, changes, access logs)
    - Technical control testing
  Output:
    - Audit report with findings
    - Major/Minor nonconformities
    - Opportunities for improvement
    - Certification recommendation (or not)

  Finding categories:
    Major Nonconformity: Certification withheld; must remediate
                         and undergo follow-up audit
    Minor Nonconformity: Certificate issued; corrective action
                         required within defined timeframe
    Observation:         Improvement opportunity; no action required

Certification Cycle (3 years):
  Year 1: Certification audit (Stage 1 + Stage 2)
  Year 2: Surveillance audit 1 (subset of controls)
  Year 3: Surveillance audit 2 (subset of controls)
  Year 4: Re-certification audit (full audit)
  ...cycle repeats

Surveillance audits:
  - Cover approximately 1/3 of ISMS scope per year
  - Must cover ALL clauses over the 3-year cycle
  - Mandatory: internal audit (9.2), management review (9.3),
    corrective actions (10.1), continual improvement (10.2)
EOF
```

## ISO 27002:2022 Implementation Guidance
```
ISO 27002 provides detailed implementation guidance for each
control in Annex A. Not auditable itself — it is a reference.

Each control in 27002:2022 includes:
  - Control title and description
  - Purpose
  - Guidance (detailed implementation advice)
  - Other information (references, considerations)

New attributes in 27002:2022 for each control:
  Control type:       Preventive, Detective, Corrective
  Info security:      Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability
  Cybersecurity:      Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover
  Operational:        Governance, Asset Mgmt, Protection, etc.
  Security domains:   Governance, Ecosystem, Protection, Defence,
                      Resilience

These attributes enable control filtering and mapping to
other frameworks (NIST CSF, CIS Controls, COBIT).
```

## Tips
- Start with the mandatory clauses (4-10) before addressing Annex A controls; the ISMS framework must be established before individual controls are selected
- Conduct a thorough gap analysis before engaging a certification body; most failed audits stem from incomplete documentation, not technical shortcomings
- Write the Statement of Applicability early and treat it as a living document; auditors will check that every Annex A control has a documented justification
- Use a 5x5 risk matrix for risk assessment but calibrate the scales to your organization; a "high" impact for a startup differs vastly from a "high" for a bank
- Plan for at least 6-12 months between project kickoff and the Stage 2 audit to allow time for controls to generate evidence of operational effectiveness
- Conduct at least one full internal audit cycle before the certification audit; internal audit findings demonstrate the "Check" phase of PDCA
- Ensure management review (Clause 9.3) is documented with inputs, decisions, and action items; it is one of the most frequently cited nonconformities
- Map your Annex A controls to other frameworks you comply with (NIST, SOC 2, PCI DSS) to reduce duplicate effort and demonstrate integrated governance
- Address the 11 new controls in the 2022 revision proactively, especially threat intelligence (5.7), cloud security (5.23), and secure coding (8.28)
- Prepare for surveillance audits by maintaining evidence continuously; cramming evidence before an audit signals weak operational maturity to auditors
